GRAMMAR

Grammar: Our Explorer's Tools

To talk about the great explorers of the history, we need two special tools in our backpack. Let's review them!

1. Past Simple: Verb To be

We use the past of the verb to be to talk about situations in the past or to say when someone was born.

Affirmative and Negative:

I, He, She, It ➡️ was / wasn't (She was born in England. He wasn't at school.)
You, We, They ➡️ were / weren't (They were excited. We weren't invited.)
Questions:

Yes/No Questions: Was he surprised? (Yes, he was / No, he wasn't). Were they bored? (Yes, they were).
Wh- Questions: Where were you yesterday? Who was a teacher?

2. Adjectives: -ed vs -ing

🚨⚠️🚨Adjectives can end in -ing or -ed, but they mean different things!🚨⚠️🚨 

Use -ed for Feelings: It describes how a person feels inside.

Example: They were so frightened! (¡Estaban muy asustados!)

Use -ing for Things or Situations: It describes the thing that causes the feeling.

Example: This film is frightening! (¡Esta película es aterradora!)

 

(Note: If you say "I'm boring", it means "soy una persona aburrida". If you say "I'm bored", it means "estoy aburrido/a").

VOCABULARY

📖 Dictionary of Exploration

Here is the vocabulary you need for your missions. Learn these words to be a great journalist and explorer!

🌍🧭🌍TRAVELLING (Viajes y lugares): 

Countries / Places: Países / Lugares.
Culture / History: Cultura / Historia.
Dangerous: Peligroso/a.
Maps: Mapas.
Travel / Trip / Journey: Viajar / Viaje corto / Travesía.
Traveller: Viajero/a.


Adjectives for Famous Explorers:                                                                                      

Brave / Courageous / Daring: Valiente / Atrevido/a.
Conquer: Conquistar.
Discover: Descubrir.
Early life: Primeros años (infancia).
Expedition: Expedición.
Pioneer: Pionero/a (the first person to do something).
Tribute: Homenaje.


General adjectives

Bored / Boring: Aburrido/a (feeling) / Aburrido/a (thing).
Excited / Exciting: Emocionado/a / Emocionante.
Fascinated / Fascinating: Fascinado/a / Fascinante.
Frightened / Frightening: Asustado/a / Aterrador o que da miedo.
Interested / Interesting: Interesado/a / Interesante.
Surprised / Surprising: Sorprendido/a / Sorprendente.
Terrified / Terrifying: Aterrorizado/a / Espeluznante.
